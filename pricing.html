Common Questions

FAQ

Why retainer-only?

E-commerce performance compounds. One-off projects deliver a snapshot fix; the value of the work shows up over months as conversion, organic traffic, and operational discipline accumulate. A retainer is the only structure honest about that. It also keeps the relationship lean: one engineer, one client, no agency overhead, no account managers translating between you and the work.

What's in a fix sprint?

A fix sprint is 2 to 3 weeks of concentrated work on the highest-leverage issues surfaced by the audit. Typical scope: Core Web Vitals and site-speed fixes, structured-data corrections, broken redirect chains and indexation problems, conversion-killing checkout issues, and platform-specific configuration mistakes. You get a written before/after report so the work is auditable, not just promised.

Do you take one-off projects?

Yes, but selectively. The fix sprint at $8,000+ is a defined one-off. Platform migrations and store-setup engagements are also one-off by nature. Outside of those, we generally don't take small one-off work because the discovery and onboarding overhead eats most of the engagement. If you have a specific, scoped project, ask and we'll tell you straight whether it's a fit.

Can I cancel anytime?

Monthly retainers run on a 6-month initial term, then month-to-month with 30 days notice. The 6-month minimum exists because the work genuinely doesn't show measurable results in 30 to 60 days. After the initial term, cancel anytime. If we're 6 months in and you don't see value, you shouldn't keep paying — and we'll tell you that before you do.
