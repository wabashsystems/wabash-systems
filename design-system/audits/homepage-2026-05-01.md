## Top 3 highest-leverage fixes

### 1. Resolve the dual-funnel problem
Pick *one* primary action: either "Book a Free Consultation" (Cal.com) or "Let's Talk" (contact form), not both. The hero has the booking CTA, then 1247 scrolls down and the intro section pushes the form CTA, then the CTA band re-pushes booking, then contact has the form. A visitor who's "ready to talk" sees the form-fill option and the booking option roughly equally, and makes the decision twice. Making the booking the only primary CTA (with a "or send a message" subdued link below it) would consolidate intent into one funnel and remove the "which one do I pick" hesitation. Strong recommendation: Cal.com booking is the primary; the form becomes a secondary "if you're not ready for a call" path.

### 2. Cut the service grid to 4 cards or group into 3 buckets
Six options is a 30%+ drop-off zone for marketing pages. Either:
**(a)** Pick the four highest-converting services and demote the other two to the services index page (linked once at the bottom: "see all 6 services"). My pick: Store Setup, Platform Migration, SEO Management, Monthly Management — those are the four that get someone to a meaningful engagement size.
**(b)** Group into 3 buckets: Build (Store Setup + Web Design), Grow (SEO + Google Ads), Run (Migration + Monthly Management). Each bucket card opens to a sub-page or modal listing the underlying services.

I'd pick (a). It's simpler and it forces a meaningful "what do I actually want clients to start with" decision.

### 3. Add one trust signal above the fold
A single client logo bar or a single testimonial under the hero stats moves Trust from 4/5 to 5/5 and Cognitive Load from 13/20 to 15/20 (less mental work to validate "is this guy real"). For now you don't have client logos, so a single line under the hero stats: *"Built by Andy Gray — 25 years running e-commerce ops at a national flooring retailer in Indianapolis."* That's a 1-sentence proof statement that tells a stranger exactly why they should trust the rest of the page.

These three fixes alone push the score from 74 to ~88. About 2 hours of work.

## Full defect list

### Critical (must fix to PASS)

**1.** Two competing primary CTAs across the page. UX.md §8 violation: "ONE primary action."
- File: `index.html:1246` (`Book a Free Consultation` → /booking)
- File: `index.html:1273` (`Let's Talk` → #contact)
- File: `index.html:1484` (`Book Your Free Call` → /booking)
- Fix: Pick one path. Demote the other to subdued secondary.

**2.** Six service cards exceeds Hick's Law sweet spot of 3–5. UX.md §6 violation: "minimise choices."
- File: `index.html:1317-1361`
- Fix: Reduce to 4 (recommended) or group into 3 buckets.

**3.** No third-party validation visible above the fold. UX.md §3 (Trust) and §11 (Trust signals).
- File: `index.html:1235-1264` (hero)
- Fix: Add one logo bar or one testimonial line, even if it's just a self-attribution proof statement.

### Important (drags score)

**4.** Hero stats compete with H1 for attention. DESIGN.md §6 ("only one dominant element allowed").
- File: `index.html:1249-1262` (three large numbers below CTAs)
- Fix: Reduce stat size by 30–40%, lighten font weight, or move stats below the hero into a strip above the services section.

**5.** Sticky bottom bar adds a fourth+ CTA after 25s. UX.md anti-pattern: "multiple primary actions."
- File: `index.html:1106-1173` (bar styles), plus the JS that triggers it
- Fix: Either remove the bar entirely (simpler) or change the trigger from time-based to intent-based (mouse leaves viewport top — exit-intent only).

**6.** Hero subtitle is 47 words in one paragraph. DESIGN.md §4: "max line length 50–80 characters; avoid dense paragraphs."
- File: `index.html:1244`
- Fix: Cut to ~25 words. "Launch, grow, and manage profitable online stores. Store setup, SEO, Google Ads — without the guesswork."

**7.** Contact form has 6–7 fields. UX.md §10: "too many fields."
- File: `index.html:1519-1572`
- Fix: Required = First Name + Email + Message. Move Last Name, Business Name, Service Interest behind a "More details (optional)" disclosure.

**8.** Three brand orange shades (`--orange #c4622d`, `--orange-light #e8834d`, `--orange-text #b05a28`). DESIGN.md §3: "primary colour = ONE purpose (main CTA only)."
- File: `index.html:90-100`
- Fix: Consolidate. `--orange` for CTAs only, `--orange-text` for text on white. Drop or rename `--orange-light` (decorative).

### Minor (polish)

**9.** Banner emoji 🚀 doesn't communicate meaning. DESIGN.md §3: "must always communicate meaning."
- File: `index.html:1183`
- Fix: Drop the emoji or replace with SVG icon related to "service area / opening soon."

**10.** About section h2 ("25 Years in the Trenches. Now Bringing It Home.") requires interpretation. UX.md Zero Context Rule.
- File: `index.html:1409`
- Fix: Either keep voice (it's distinctive) and accept the ~1s parse cost, or shift to plain framing: "25 years of e-commerce operations — now serving small businesses locally."

**11.** Service card icons are emoji rather than SVG. DESIGN.md §7: consistency.
- File: `index.html:1320, 1327, 1334, 1341, 1348, 1355` (one per card)
- Fix: Switch to a consistent inline SVG icon set (Heroicons, Lucide, or hand-drawn). Lower priority — emojis render OK across modern browsers but vary in style by OS, which is the consistency issue.

**12.** "Online Revenue Machine" hero phrase is metaphor. UX.md Zero Context Rule.
- File: `index.html:1243`
- Fix: Optional. The phrase has voice but a small-business owner skimming has to do a "wait, what?" beat. Alternative: "Turn Your Small Business Into a Real Online Store."

## What's working — don't change

- **Pricing visible on every service card.** Rare on consulting sites. Massive trust + qualification signal.
- **Local-first framing.** Naming Robinson, Lawrenceville, Mt. Carmel, Olney specifically beats generic "Midwest" by miles.
- **Process section is excellent.** Four numbered steps, clear progression, no fluff.
- **About section specificity.** "$700–800K/month managed revenue" + Magento + BigCommerce + ERP integrations — concrete and verifiable.
- **Skip nav, ARIA labels, lang attribute, focus styles, mobile drawer.** Accessibility scaffolding is solid.
- **Performance work.** Self-hosted Inter, deferred Sentry/PostHog, no Klaviyo bloat. Page is genuinely fast.
- **Schema.org LocalBusiness markup** with city-by-city `areaServed` is excellent local SEO.
- **Color contrast comments** in CSS (`/* Darker orange for text on light backgrounds — passes 4.5:1 on white */`). Shows accessibility was thought about during design, not bolted on after.
