Lawrenceville Today

A border town with two-state customer reach

Most Lawrenceville businesses already know that customers don't stop at the river. People drive in from Vincennes for everyday errands and drive across to Indiana for groceries, gas, and weekend retail. The internet doesn't change that pattern — it amplifies it. When somebody in Bridgeport searches for a service, or when a Knox County resident is comparing options, the businesses that show up first in those results are the ones that win the visit.

The hard part is that "showing up" online isn't a one-time job anymore. Google changes how local results work every few months, social platforms keep moving, and a five-year-old website probably doesn't render right on a phone. We help small Lawrenceville businesses catch up to where the search behavior already is — without overspending or buying things that don't apply to a town this size.

First conversation is free, takes 30 minutes, and there's no pitch deck. We'd rather tell you the one thing we'd actually fix first than try to sell you a six-figure rebuild.
