The Problem

Staying on Magento gets more expensive every month.

Magento is powerful, but the cost of ownership keeps climbing. Each of these tends to show up quietly — and then all at once.

  • Magento 1 Enterprise is EOL. Officially dead since June 2020. Security patches are third-party, extensions are abandoned, and the pool of developers willing to work on it shrinks every year.
  • Magento 2 hosting, extensions, and maintenance eat margin. A typical M2 store runs $800–$3,000/month just to stay operational — before you add new features.
  • Speed problems hurt conversion and paid traffic ROI. Heavy code, bloated checkout, slow product pages. Google Ads Quality Score drops. Organic traffic suffers from Core Web Vitals penalties.
  • Your team can't ship. Every change needs a developer. Simple merchandising takes a week. Marketing promotions take longer to launch than they run.
  • PCI and security compliance keeps getting harder. Especially with M1. One framework CVE and you're scrambling.

None of that is catastrophic on its own. All of them at once is a signal the platform has outlived its fit.

The Process

Three phases, no surprises.

Most failed migrations skip discovery or rush cutover. The process below is the one that ran a $10M flooring retailer off Magento 1 Enterprise with zero downtime — the same approach applies to smaller stores.

Phase 1

Discovery & mapping

Full audit of your existing store — every integration, cron, custom module, workflow. Decide what moves, what gets replaced, what gets retired.

~4 weeks
Phase 2

Rebuild & parallel run

New platform stood up alongside Magento. Data sync, theme rebuild, integration work. You see and test the new store well before cutover.

8–16 weeks
Phase 3

Cutover & harden

Final sync, DNS swap, redirect map live. Post-launch monitoring through a full business cycle. Rollback ready the whole time.

2–4 weeks

Real-world proof: This is the exact approach that moved a $10M online flooring retailer off Magento 1 Enterprise in nine months. Zero downtime. No SEO loss. Revenue held through cutover. Three years later the store is still on the new platform.

Common Questions

What people ask before pulling the trigger.

How long does a full migration take?

Most projects land between 4 and 9 months, depending on catalog size, integration complexity, and your team's availability. Discovery takes about a month. Rebuild is the long phase — it scales with the amount of custom work. Cutover itself is usually a single weekend.

Will my SEO rankings take a hit?

Only if redirects are done poorly. On the projects I've led, the full 301 redirect map is built before go-live, including legacy category paths and edge cases. Organic traffic stayed flat through the cutover on the $10M migration — no recovery period.

What will it cost?

Fixed-fee engagements for full migrations typically land between $25,000 and $120,000 depending on scope. You'll get an itemized quote after the discovery call — not hourly estimates that balloon.

What about my data — orders, customers, history?

Everything that lives in Magento can be migrated: products, variants, categories, customers, order history, reviews, loyalty records. We validate against production data before cutover so no numbers surprise your finance team post-launch.

BigCommerce, Shopify, or WooCommerce — which one?

The right answer depends on catalog size, B2B vs B2C, integration complexity, and where your team wants to spend time. We don't push one. The recommendation comes out of the discovery phase, with reasons, not opinions.

Who actually does the work?

I (Andy Gray) lead every engagement personally — this isn't an agency handoff to junior devs. For larger projects I bring in a small team of engineers I've worked with for years. You get the same people from discovery through cutover.
